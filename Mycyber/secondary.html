SECONDARY MEMORY

This is a storage device that offers a permanent data storage in the computer, they are either inbuilt or external devices, the main advantage of these memories is that they are non volatile and hence data stored on them is not lost when the power goes off and hence offering a proper storage files for a longer period of time. Some of the main storage components in a computer include:

Hard disk

image courtesy of indiamart

It is a storage device that uses magnetic storage to store and retrieve the data that is stored in it. This type storage device contains some round shaped magnetic platters where data is stored ,at the center of the magnetic disks is a spindle that helps rotate the read/write head for reading and data retrieval. These memories come with different sizes and it is one of the commonly used secondary storage devices where the Operating System is loaded. The main drawbacks of these memories are they are very slow due to the rotation of read/write hand and also very noisy.

Solid State Memories

image courtesy of extremetech

The solid state memories comes as an advanced form of the hard disks due to the fact that they do not have moving component as are very fast and not noisy and consume a less amount of heat.

Flash disks

image courtesy of digistore kenya

A storage device used to store data and is connected to the computer through the USB ports, It is a play and plug memory and hence the most commonly used external storage device due to its small size and also the portability factor of it.

SD Cards (memory cards)

image courtesy of cello electronics

A short form of Secure Digital Card. A storage device mainly used in mobile phones and digital cameras. For one to connect the memory to a computer he/she has to connect through a SD card reader. However these memories have started becoming outdated due to presence of mobile phones with higher internal storages.

Compact disks

image courtesy of wikipedia

These are circular disks that use optical rays especially the laser rays to read and white data, these disks are connected to the computer through a CD Drive found on the system unit. They are of three types namely.
  • CD ROM - data stored on these devices cannot be written into and is only read. An example of these CD’s is those used to store proprietary software’s and computer games software’s.
  • CD –Recordable (CDR) – data once stored in this CD’s cannot be deleted or modified later, an example of these CD’s are those used to store music.
  • CD – Rewritable – data stored in these devices cannot be written or deleted.

    • Digital Versatile Disk (DVD)

    image courtesy of wikipedia

    Used to store large multimedia files since they are higher space than that of CD’s, They are mainly used for high content movies and videos.
